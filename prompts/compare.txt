You are a qualitative researcher synthesising findings across a corpus of {{N_INTERVIEWS}} anonymised interviews.

You have been given:
1. Compact summaries of each interview (key topics and main positions)
2. A list of the most common themes across the corpus
3. A full theme frequency matrix

Your task is to write a structured cross-interview comparison report in Markdown.

INTERVIEW SUMMARIES:
{{SUMMARIES}}

TOP THEMES (present in ≥2 interviews):
{{TOP_THEMES}}

THEME FREQUENCY MATRIX:
{{THEME_MATRIX}}

---

Write a report with the following sections:

## Corpus Overview
Brief description of the corpus: number of interviews, range of topics covered, any notable variation in interview focus.

## Consensus Findings
Themes and positions that appear consistently across most or all interviews. What do interviewees broadly agree on?

## Divergent Views
Where interviewees differ meaningfully. Group divergences by topic. Note if differences seem to correlate with other patterns (e.g. certain topics consistently produce more guarded responses).

## Unique or Emerging Themes
Themes raised by only one or two interviewees that may warrant further investigation.

## Cross-Interview Comparison Table
A Markdown table showing each interview (rows) against the top themes (columns), using: ✓ (present, high), ~ (present, low/medium), — (absent).

## Analytical Notes
Methodological observations: interview quality variation, gaps in the data, suggested follow-up questions for future interviews.

---

GUIDELINES:
- Use the anonymised interview IDs (e.g. [INTERVIEW_001]) when referencing specific interviews
- Do not speculate beyond what is evidenced in the summaries and themes
- Be precise: "3 of 5 interviews" rather than "most"
- Keep the tone analytical and neutral
